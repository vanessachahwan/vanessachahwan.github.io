Can't Hurt Me

David Goggins

Key Concepts

Accountability Mirror

Set goals, write them on Post-It notes. And each day, hold yourself accountable to the goals you'd set.

Goal Planning

List the necessary steps to achieve your goals day by day, each step should be a Post-It. Stay focused and do some research to break it all down. Whatever the goal is, hold yourself accountable for the small steps it will take to get there.

Calloused Mind

Step outside your comfort zone on a regular basis, do the things you don't like to do or that make you uncomfortable, and that are good for you. Do first and dig for more. Turn your weaknesses into strengths.

Past Experiences

Go back through your past and make peace with yourself by facing the incidents and all of your negative influences, and accepting them as weak spots in your own character. Only when you identify and accept your weaknesses will you finally stop running from your past. These incidents can be utilized more effectively as motivation to become better and grow stronger.

Armored Mind

Choose obstacles, set new goals, and visualize overcoming or achieving them. Visualize the challenges that are likely to arise and determine how you will approach and conquer those challenges when they emerge. That way, you will be as prepared as possible on the journey. Visualize success as well.

Cookie Jar

List all your achievements, life obstacles that you've overcome and success tastes even a small win. Store them in a cookie jar. When life gets hard or when you are challenged, pull a cookie and let it fuel you.

Challenge Yourself

Set ambitious goals and when something hits pull a cookie to fuel.

The 40% Rule

Overcome the mental limitations and push past your normal stopping point, even when you feel discomfort and believe you've given only 40% of your maximum effort.

Talent Not Required

If you want to master the mind and remove governor, you'll have to become addicted to hard work. Because passion and obsession, even talent, are only useful tools if you have the work ethic to back them up.

Schedule Audit

Document your schedule and build an optimal schedule and adapt. When you work, only work on one thing at a time, think about the task in front of you and pursue it relentlessly. Schedule time for exercise and rest.

Uncommon Among Uncommon

Sustain greatness for a long period of time, stay in constant pursuit and put unending effort. Start at zero after achieving a goal, refrigerator is never full and will never be. You should never feel that your work is done. There is always more to do.

Failure Live Autopsy

For failures, write all things that went well and your thinking during failure, and make a list of things you can fix fix, then make a tactical plan.
